EchoRisk-MICCAI

MICCAI 2026 · Satellite Challenge

EchoRisk-MICCAI AI for Cardiac Function Estimation & Early Cardiotoxicity Prediction The first MICCAI challenge focused on AI-driven detection and early prediction of therapy–induced cardiotoxicity from echocardiography, built on real-world, multicenter data from 421 breast cancer patients.



// Team

Why EchoRisk?

Cardiotoxicity is a critical complication of breast cancer therapy. Up to 20–30% of patients receiving anthracyclines and 7–10% receiving trastuzumab develop some form of cardiac dysfunction — yet early signs are frequently missed.

Echocardiography is the standard of care for cardiac monitoring, but manual analysis is operator-dependent, time-consuming, and subject to significant inter-observer variability.
EchoRisk-MICCAI provides the first curated, multicenter, longitudinal echocardiography dataset with explicit cardiotoxicity labels — enabling standardized AI benchmarking in cardio-oncology.

Built on the EU-funded CARDIOCARE project, the dataset captures real-world clinical heterogeneity across 6 European sites in 5 countries.

EchoRisk-MICCAI is an official MICCAI 2026 challenge, with participation, submissions, and evaluation conducted through Synapse.

Clinical Need
Subclinical Dysfunction Goes Undetected
Manual echocardiographic assessment is highly variable, limiting early detection at scale in longitudinal cancer monitoring programs.

Technical Gap
No Cardiotoxicity-Specific Benchmark
Existing echocardiography challenges focus on generic cardiac function. No public dataset integrates therapy-induced cardiotoxicity labels with longitudinal imaging.

Our Contribution
Three Clinically Grounded Tasks
From functional parameter estimation to early risk prediction — tasks aligned with real clinical decision-making in cardio-oncology workflows.
